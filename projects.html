Why you should join BHKi

University/Campus Outreaches

The goal of the outreaches stems from BHKi's primary objective, which is to close the knowledge gap between early-career researchers and bioinformatics experts. In this regard, we aim to sensitize and educate undergraduates about bioinformatics and open science. Our target is universities offering life science and computer science courses at the undergraduate level and the program encompasses brief introductions to bioinformatics and open science, the trends in the fields, and use cases worldwide.

Science Cafes and Tool Hub

The Science Cafe is a monthly virtual presentation done by our members of their work. The tool hub is a technical series following the science cafes that involves training and presentation of various bioinformatics tools to do different analyses. The aim of these events is to encourage members to share their work and embrace collaboration with experts and other researchers.


Capacity Building: Empowering researchers skills workshops and seminars

We need properly skilled researchers to carry out research and come up with solutions specific to our regions. The goal of our training is to impart researchers with bioinformatics skills they require to carry out research projects and produce useful output. Our workshops are centered around:

  1. Programming: Python, R, Bash scripting
  2. Data analysis: Metagenomics, Phylogenetics, transcriptomics, epigenetics, and more.
  3. Publishing and presenting scientific research.
We also develop curriculum for training Check out some of our training on our YouTube channel.

BioHackFiesta: Monthly Bioinformatics Codeathons

Practice makes perfect! The BioHackFiesta is a series of hackathons we will be doing to encourage innovation and out of the box thinking in our members.
