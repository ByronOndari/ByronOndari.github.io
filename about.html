Leadership

Our team comprises of Bioinformaticians speacializing in different fields of the field but with a common enthusiasm for capacity building and community development. We believe in community as a way to propagate bioinformatics research and education to build more region specificÂ solutions.

Festus Nyasimi

Chairperson BHKi

Festus is a Bioinformatician and a Data Scientist using statistical tools and computational methods to understand the genetics of complex diseases and traits. He is a cofounder of BHKi. With formal training in Bioinformatics, Health Research methods, Microbiology, and Biotechnology, Festus actively participates in training sessions and community outreach initiatives in the region. He has previously served as a trainer for the Africa Advance Bioinformatics Network (AABN), mentored the second cohort of HackBio Internship, and remains actively involved in the Open Life Science (OLS) community. Festus has also contributed to noteworthy research projects at esteemed institutions like ICIPE and KWTRP. Outside of work, he finds joy in traveling and seeking out adventurous experiences.

Karega Pauline

Vice Chairperson

Pauline Karega is a bioinformatics student and researcher with experience i n evolutionary biology. Her research interests are in health data and health information systems. She is a co-founder of the Bioinformatics Hub of Kenya initiative (BHKi) and enjoys working at a project management capacity in community initiatives. She is passionate about capacity building in bioinformatics and data science, and community outreach. Outside science Pauline enjoys performing arts such as musicals and dance festivals.

Margaret Wanjiru

Organizing Secretary

Margaret serves the role of secretary to the BHKI steering committee. She is a bioinformatician with a keen interest in omics and big data analysis.

Michael Landi

Organizing Lead

Michael is a bioinformatics student with a keen interest in epigenetics and genomics, currently a PhD student at the Swedish University of Agricultural Sciences. A co-founder of Bioinformatics Hub of Kenya (BHKI). He is a certified Software Carpentries instructor, an Open Life Science (OLS) graduate and a mentor. He is passionate about capacity building. Hobbies: Triathlon!

David Kiragu

Finance lead

David is a bioinformatics student with a keen interest in both genomic data production and using computational skill sets to answer research questions in the fields of zoonotic diseases and wildlife conservation. He is a BiXCop 2021 fellow and has a background in Bioinformatics, Molecular Biology and Microbiology. Due to this special interest in capacity development and community outreach, he is also a co-founder and trainer at Bioinformatics Hub of Kenya Initiative(BHKi) and African International Biotechnology and Biomedical Conference(AIBBC). His current affiliation is at the Institute of Primate Research(IPR) as a researcher, Kenya. Fun activities: Cycling and travelling.

Laurah Ondari

Communications Lead

Nyasita (she/her) is a science enthusiast in the fields of bioinformatics, computer-aided drug design, and medical biochemistry. Nyasita holds an MSc in Bioinformatics and with a strong background in omics data analysis and in silico modeling, she is dedicated to leading and contributing to projects focused on leveraging bioinformatics techniques to discover disease cures. She also hosts the Science In Real Life podcast (SIRL), where she brings science stories to life. Additionally, Nyasita is part of the Open Life Science where she serves in the governance committee and mentors others. In her spare time, she volunteers in community development and capacity building efforts.
